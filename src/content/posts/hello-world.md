After a few years publishing on Medium, I've moved.

Not because Medium is bad — it isn't. The editor is great, the audience discovery is useful, and I've found real readers there. But over time I kept running into the same quiet frustration: the reading experience isn't really mine to shape, the URLs aren't mine, and there's a slow creep of friction between the idea in my head and the thing a reader actually sees.

So I built this instead.

## What this is

A blog. Nothing more, nothing less. Posts written in Markdown, rendered to clean HTML, served as static files from a host I control. No tracking pixels, no engagement metrics, no algorithm nudging me toward hot takes.

The design is deliberately minimal — mostly a good typeface, generous whitespace, and content that has room to breathe. I've tried to make it accessible out of the box: semantic HTML, proper heading hierarchy, readable contrast, keyboard navigable.

## What I'll write about

Same things I always have: ideas I'm turning over, things I've built, observations about how technology and culture rub against each other. Probably some personal essays when the mood takes me.

The pace will be unhurried. There's no publish-or-perish pressure here, no algorithm to feed. If something takes three months to be ready, it takes three months.

## For the technically curious

The stack is [Astro](https://astro.build) with Markdown content collections — static output, zero JavaScript by default. Hosted on the edge. The fonts are Lora and Inter from Google Fonts. The whole thing fits in a single repository and deploys in about 30 seconds.

I'll probably write more about the build at some point, but for now: it works, it's fast, and it's mine.
