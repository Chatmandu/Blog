I've been writing on Medium for a few years. It's a decent platform. The editor is clean, the distribution is real, and it got my work in front of people who wouldn't have found it otherwise.

But something about it started to feel off.

Not wrong, exactly. Just a version of the thing all of us who write online eventually notice: you're not really writing on your own terms. You're writing for an algorithm, on someone else's real estate, against a clock that measures success in minutes read rather than ideas landed. Medium's goals and your goals are related, but they're not the same. And over time, that gap starts to show.

So I built this.

---

### What this is

This site is [manualmode.xyz](https://manualmode.xyz) — a personal blog, built from scratch, owned outright, going nowhere.

I write essays. Mostly about marketing, technology, and AI, and the places where those things collide with culture, communication, and how organisations actually work. Sometimes I write about design. Sometimes about a book, a film, a football result, or a thing my kids said that turned out to be more interesting than I expected.

The thread connecting most of it: I'm trying to think clearly about things that move fast, and I find writing the best way to do that. So this is the thinking out loud.

---

### What you'll find here

All the posts I published on Medium are here, brought over and given a proper home. The oldest ones go back to May 2025. Everything from here on will live here first.

The subjects I keep coming back to:

**AI as a practical tool, not a news story.** I'm less interested in whether AI will take jobs than in what it actually does when you put it to work. I've been building things with it, using it to run analysis I couldn't afford to run otherwise, and trying to understand where it genuinely helps and where it flatters to deceive.

**How organisations communicate.** I've spent most of my career in higher education and the public sector. These are places where communication often looks like it's working while quietly failing. That gap between intent and reception is endlessly interesting to me.

**The craft of making things.** Writing, design, building — the process of taking something from idea to finished thing. Not productivity advice. The actual texture of it.

---

### Why manual mode

The name is about the thing I've been thinking about most lately: the choice between handing control to a system and keeping it yourself.

Manual mode on a camera gives you a worse photo in some situations and a much better one in others. The difference is whether you know what you're doing and what you're trying to say. That tension — between automation and intention, between efficiency and craft — shows up everywhere I'm interested in. Marketing, AI, design, communication. It seemed like a good name for a place that tries to think that through.
